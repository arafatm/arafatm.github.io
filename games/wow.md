## TLDR Lore for each Expansion

### The Burning Crusade

The Dark Portal reopens, allowing passage to the shattered world of Draenor.
And the intrepid adventures of Azeroth conclude that where there are demons and
hellspawn, there must be loot, so to Outland(Draenor) we go.

While in the neighborhood we decide this may be a good opportunity to deal with
Prince Kael'thas a naughty elf who's been draining the wetlands netherstorm.

Moving on to the next target in our Warcraft 3 hero hit-list; we roll over to
the Black Temple to kill Illidan. Another naughty elf who has recently
remodeled much of the place into a demon resort and spa.

Meanwhile back on Azeroth, a group of Draenei from Outland perform the
traditional greeting ceremony of crashing their space ship into a night elf
village. Thus cementing their friendship and admittance into the Alliance.

Kael'thas, having since realized that Illidan was not the father figure he
imagined, returns to his homeland to summon the great demon Kil'jaden.
Unfortunately however, Kil'jaden is too fat to fully fit through the portal and
is heroically repelled. Kael'thas is also killed for good this time we think.

### Wrath of the Lich King

5 years since his crowning as prom king of the undead, Arthas the Lich King
awakes from his long nap with the intent of wiping out all life on Azeroth.
Starting with the Paladins of the Argent Dawn, who he entasks his troupe of
Death Knights to wipe out. But in a completely expected turn of events, the
Death Knights are instead are taught the true value of friendship and join
forces with the Argent Dawn, forming the Argent Crusade.

The Argent Crusade then decides that the best coarse of action against Arthas,
is to hold a series of gladiatorial & olympic games right outside his doorstep
and not invite him.

Once the coliseum participants grew tired of deep fried fair food and
mechanical kodo rides. The Argent Crusade takes the fight to Arthas himself,
who is unceremoniously dispatched with the help of a few Saronite Bombs. With
Arthas dead, the Human Torch moves in to take up the crown of the Lich King,
informing Tirion, lord of the Argent Crusade, that the world must never know
what happened. Ignoring this, Tirion builds a statue of himself in the middle
of town telling the world what happened.

### Cataclysm

Azeroth collectively rolls a 1 on their luck check releasing Deathwing the
Worldbreaker from his sabbatical in Deepholme. Deathwing marks his triumphant
return with an aggressive landscaping job that destroys much of the world.

Thrall nopes out of this mess and hops a greyhound to his private getaway in
Nagrand. But not before promoting his friend from anger management counciling,
Garrosh, to Warchief of the Horde. A decision that is met with criticism from
some, and trial by combat from others.

Having recently discovered a loophole in the Hague convention on chemical
weapons. Sylvanas marshals her undead forces to break down the hard Gilnean
wall to get to the chewy nougat-worgen inside. But once again the Night Elves
are there in time to make friends and recruit the Worgen into the Alliance,
before their imminent demise.

Not to be outdone the Horde co-opts a mine field* of wayward goblins into the
ranks of the Horde.

*Mine field being the collective noun for a group of Goblins.

Returning from his private retreat, Thrall takes a Delorean back in time to
steal the one artifact that can defeat Deathwing. With the help of the
Planeteers Dragon Aspects, and some incredibly patient players. Captain Planet
Thrall uses the artifact to drop a spirit bomb on Deathwing, saving the world
and aren't we glad for him.

### Mists of Pandaria

I'll be honest, I didn't play this expansion. But my understanding is that
Garrosh goes a bit mental, succumbs to the will of an old god, and is thrown in
panda prison on charges of genocide and two overdue parking tickets. And it's
up to Garrosh's old partners in crime to come out of retirement for one last
job, to bust him out.

### Warlords of Draenor

Once out of prison, Garrosh takes a portal back in time & space to the Orc
homeworld of Draenor before it got flipped-turned upside down. After a brief
family reunion involving the death of an Annihilan Pit Lord. Garrosh unites the
past Orc clans in a plot to invade future Azeroth.

This went relatively well for the first 12 or so steps out of the Dark Portal.
Before the forces of Azeroth descended on the new Iron Horde in such numbers
that the servers are still attending therapy to cope with the experience.

While the armies of Azeroth took the fight to the Iron Horde, Thrall challenges
Garrosh to a WWE championship cagefight. However due to a misunderstanding of
the rules, Garrosh shows up in only his underwear, while Thrall comes fully
equiped with plate armor, mystic elemental powers, and an M1 Abrams Battle
Tank.

After seeing most of the Iron Horde defeated resident fel-enthusiast Gul'dan
decides to do what he do and summon the demon lord Archimonde. Who, unlike his
colleague, has no trouble fitting through the portal. Players enlist the aid of
new-friends Yrel & Durotan, as well as an Apexis addicted Khadgar, to defeat
the demon lord and save past-Draenor. Sadly however, before his death,
Archimonde tosses Gul'dan into a nearby portal which sends him back to the
future-Azeroth, where our wild and wacky story waits to be continued!
